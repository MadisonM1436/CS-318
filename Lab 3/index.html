History of Timekeeping

Time has been measured for centuries using different methods. Early civilizations used **sundials**, hourglasses, and water clocks to track the passing of time.

The invention of mechanical clocks in the 14th century revolutionized timekeeping, leading to modern digital and atomic clocks.

Units of Time

  • 1 Second = The base unit of time.
  • 1 Minute = 60 seconds.
  • 1 Hour = 60 minutes.
  • 1 Day = 24 hours.
  • 1 Year = 365 days (or 366 in leap years).

Fun Facts About Time

  1. A day on Venus is longer than a year on Venus
  2. Atomic clocks are so precise they only lose 1 second every 100 million years.
  3. The idea of a leap year was introduced by Julius Caesar in 45 BC.
  4. Time slows down as you approach the speed of light (thanks, Einstein!).
  5. The world's oldest clock is over 600 years old and still works!
