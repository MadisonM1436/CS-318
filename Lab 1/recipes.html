Beef Bourguignon

Ingredients

  • 2 lbs beef chuck, cut into cubes
  • 4 slices bacon, chopped
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 2 carrots, sliced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 cups red wine
  • 1 cup beef broth
  • 1 tbsp tomato paste
  • 1 tsp thyme
  • 1 tbsp flour
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. In a large pot, cook the bacon over medium heat until crispy. Remove and set aside.
  2. Season the beef with salt and pepper, then sear in the bacon fat until browned on all sides.
  3. Add the onions, carrots, and garlic. Cook until softened.
  4. Stir in the tomato paste and flour, then add the wine and broth. Bring to a simmer.
  5. Return the bacon to the pot, add thyme, and cover. Simmer for 2-3 hours until the beef is tender.
  6. Serve hot with mashed potatoes or crusty bread.

Spaghetti Carbonara

Ingredients

  • 12 oz spaghetti
  • 4 oz pancetta or bacon, diced
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1 tsp black pepper
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tbsp olive oil

Instructions

  1. Cook the spaghetti in a large pot of salted boiling water until al dente. Reserve 1 cup of pasta water before draining.
  2. In a large pan, heat olive oil over medium heat and cook pancetta until crispy. Add garlic and sauté for 1 minute.
  3. In a bowl, whisk together eggs, Parmesan cheese, salt, and black pepper.
  4. Remove the pan from heat, then quickly toss in the drained spaghetti. Gradually add the egg mixture while stirring vigorously to create a creamy sauce.
    If needed, add reserved pasta water a little at a time to reach the desired consistency.
  5. Serve immediately with extra Parmesan cheese and black pepper on top.

Spicy Vodka Pasta

Ingredients

  • ½ lb orecchiette (or your favorite pasta shape)
  • 4 tbsp butter
  • 1 small red onion, finely diced
  • Salt and fresh ground pepper, to taste
  • 6 garlic cloves, thinly sliced or minced
  • ½ cup tomato paste
  • ¼ cup vodka
  • 1 ½ tsp red pepper flakes
  • 2 cups heavy cream
  • ¼ cup reserved pasta water
  • 1 cup freshly grated Parmesan cheese (plus more for serving)
  • Fresh basil leaves, for serving

Instructions

  1. Boil pasta according to package directions for al dente. Salt the water well before cooking the pasta (it should taste like the ocean).
  2. Heat butter over medium-low heat in a large, deep sauté pan.
  3. Add the onions and season with salt and pepper. Cook, stirring occasionally, until softened, about 5-7 minutes.
  4. Add the garlic, stir and cook until just fragrant, about 1 minute.
  5. Add the tomato paste and cook, stirring occasionally, until darkened in color, about 5 minutes.
  6. Add the vodka and cook until evaporated, about 1 minute.
  7. Add the heavy cream and red pepper flakes. Cook, stirring occasionally, for 2 minutes, until slightly thickened.
  8. Add the drained cooked pasta and reserved pasta water, and toss to coat.
  9. Turn off heat, add Parmesan, and stir.
  10. Serve with additional grated Parmesan and fresh basil leaves.
